⚽️ Project 01 — Shot Analysis & xG (World Cup 2018 Final)
🎯 Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze shot patterns, expected goals (xG), and finishing efficiency in the 2018 World Cup Final between France and Croatia.

📊 Data
Source: StatsBomb Open Data
Match: France vs Croatia (World Cup Final 2018)
Data type: Event-level (shots, locations, xG)

⚙️ Methodology
Filtered shot events from match data
Extracted shot coordinates (x, y)
Visualized shot locations on pitch
Analyzed xG distribution
Compared shot volume and efficiency by team
Evaluated player-level performance

📈 Visualizations
Shot map
Goals vs misses
xG distribution by team
Player efficiency table

🧠 Key Insights
Croatia generated more shots (15 vs 8), showing higher attacking volume
However, France produced similar total xG, indicating better shot quality
France was more efficient, converting fewer chances into goals
Croatia relied more on open play, while France capitalized on a penalty
High-quality chances were concentrated near the center of the penalty area

🏁 Conclusion

This analysis highlights a key concept in football analytics:

Shot quality and efficiency are often more important than shot volume.

France’s ability to generate high-value chances and finish efficiently was decisive in securing victory.
